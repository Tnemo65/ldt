| Metric | Thesis (2024) | Actual (2025) | Discrepancy |
|--------|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| Total records (12-month) | ~30M (simulated) | **48.7M** | +62% |

| NULL rate (5-col avg) | 9.94% | **23.83%** | +2.4x |
| Jan NULL rate (passenger_count) | 4.73% | **15.54%** | +3.3x |
| Sep NULL rate (passenger_count) | 13.31% | **25.10%** | +1.9x |
| fare_amount < 0 rate | 1.78% | **5.85%** | +3.3x |
| Zero-distance rate | 1.89% | **2.88%** | +1.5x |
| Duration=0 count | 11,935 | **544,069** | +46x |
| Schema rejection rate | 10.1% | **15.54%** | +1.5x |

### Conclusion:
The 2025 simulated data has **significantly worse data quality** than the 2024 real data described in the thesis.
NULL rates are 2–3x higher, fare anomalies are 3x higher, and zero-distance trips are 1.5x more frequent.
This likely reflects the GMM-based simulation injecting more extreme/corrupted values than the thesis baseline projected.
The monthly volume is also 35% higher than the thesis projected daily average would suggest.
